N. No Distance is Too Far Apart

Alice and Bob are standing somewhere in a vaccination queue of $N$ people (including themselves). With everyone wearing facemasks and keeping their distance, it's almost impossible to recognize anyone else.

Alice counts $A$ people standing in front of her, while Bob counts $B$ people standing behind him.

Even if they can't see each other, they still want to know how far apart they are. Can you help them find out how many people are standing between Alice and Bob (excluding both of them)?

Input
One line contains three integers $N$, $A$, and $B$ $(2 \le N \le 1000, 0 \le A,B \le N-1; A+B \neq N-1)$ — the number of people in the queue, the number of people in front of Alice, and the number of people behind Bob, respectively.

Output
Output a single integer — the number of people standing between Alice and Bob.

Constraints
time limit per test 1 second
memory limit per test 256 megabytes
