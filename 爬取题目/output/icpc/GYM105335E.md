E. Executive's Holidays

A company is in the process of designing a groundbreaking new game and has decided to hold several executive meetings to kick off the project.

Over the next $t$ days, the company needs to schedule $n$ executives to attend these meetings. On any given day, $i$, the company requires at least $a_i$ executives to be present.

Each executive's "satisfaction" is determined by the length of their longest continuous break from attending meetings. For instance, if an executive attends meetings on days $1$, $5$, and $7$ during a $7$ -day period, their satisfaction value would be 3, representing the break they get from day $2$ to day $4$. If an executive does not attend any meetings during the entire period, their satisfaction value would be $t$.

You have been tasked by the CEO of the company to determine the maximum total satisfaction of all executives.

Input
The first line contains two integers $n,t$ $(1 \le n,t \le 2,000,000)$ denoting the number of executives in total and the number of days of meetings. The second line contains $t$ integers $a_1, a_2,...,a_t$ $(0 \le a_i \le n)$ — the minimum number of executives required on the $i$ -th day.

Output
Output the maximum sum of satisfaction of all the executives.

Constraints
time limit per test 1 second
memory limit per test 512 megabytes
